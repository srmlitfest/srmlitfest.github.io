Gender and Society

Parallel Session

  • Ms. Sreebitha

  • Ms.Mini Krishnan

  • Mr. Afeef Mohammad

Virginia Woolf in her Room of One’s Own stresses on the importance of having financial independence and the need for space in a woman’s life. It has been almost 90 years since her literary work has been published and yet, we are in search of that space. Feminism has taken different turns and twist in the past years, but the fundamental need and rights are still a struggle. The otherification of women and genders other than the ‘man’ continues to exist and triumph till date. The society has been and continues to be patriarchal. We come together as a group to discuss and debate issues regarding gender and feminism in this room of our own. We have called it ‘gender’ as we as a group don’t wish to discriminate against anyone. Let us together march towards an equal space.

IDENTITY, REPRESENTATION AND REALITY

Parallel Session

  • Mr. Harjinder Singh (Laltu)

  • Mr. Tenzin Tsundue

  • Mr. Bodh Prakash

  • Mr. T.D Ramakrishnan

Identity is deeply rooted in one’s culture and self. Every individual possesses multiple identities at various spaces. But beyond the identity that we possess, there is a certain manner in which our identity is being represented. This representation reaches us through movies, literature and various forms of media. And this representation is what develops into a ‘common sense’ that Antonio Gramsci speaks about. It develops as a common, accepted reality. Artists around the world, including writers and poets too, are trying to break barriers and bring representation closer to reality. Literature reflects the society to a great extent, but which strata of the society does this refraction pass through must be questioned. Let us all come together, ask questions, discuss, dissent and debate.
